Frequently Asked Questions

Who is conducting this study?

The Surfrider Foundation is conducting the study in partnership with Ecotrust, Monmouth University’s Urban Coast Institute, and The Nature Conservancy, in close coordination with the Mid-Atlantic Regional Council on the Ocean (MARCO).

What is MARCO and how does it relate to this study?

The Mid-Atlantic Regional Council on the Ocean, or MARCO, was formed by the Governors of New York, New Jersey, Delaware, Maryland, and Virginia to collaborate in advancing goals to protect our ocean and coasts. To further these goals, MARCO oversaw the development of the Mid-Atlantic Ocean Data Portal, an online tool created to help the public visualize and analyze coastal and ocean data. The data from this study will be placed on the Portal. MARCO is also helping to lead a similar survey focused solely on recreational boating.

Who should fill out the survey?

Beach goers, swimmers, beach wildlife viewers, surfers, kayakers, windsurfers, divers, and other “non-consumptive” recreational users should complete the survey. Separate studies are focusing on uses like fishing and boating.

How do I participate?

Participation is simple: go to http://www.surfrider.org/mid-atlantic-recreation and enter your email address. A unique survey link will then be sent to you via email. You can then fill out the survey at your own pace, saving as you go.

Why should I participate?

Your help is needed to ensure that your favorite recreation areas are considered in coastal and ocean planning. Recreational uses are poorly mapped and data is limited. To ensure that the interests and safety of recreational users are considered in management plans, better information is needed.

What is regional ocean planning?

As new coastal and ocean uses develop in the Mid-Atlantic, it is becoming clear that a more coordinated and thoughtful approach to planning and management is needed. Regional ocean planning gives stakeholders the opportunity to craft forward-looking plans with state and federal resource managers that reduce conflicts and help ensure our ocean resources continue to provide the benefits that people want and need. Regional ocean planning does not create new management authorities but rather provides guidance to agencies to meet planning goals using their existing authorities.

Will my favorite recreation spot or personal imformation be revealed to everyone else?

No, the data used to produce maps will be aggregated so as to not reveal individual locations. Personal data such as age and ethnicity will also be presented in aggregate form. Emails will not be shared.
