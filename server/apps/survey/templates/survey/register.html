Frequently Asked Questions

Nation-wide coastal tourism & recreation drives $70 billion to the U.S. annually. It accounts for 1.7 million jobs, and it is part of our culture, providing enjoyment for the millions who visit our coasts each year.

What is Coastal and Marine Spatial Planning (CMSP)?

Coastal and marine spatial planning is a method used to coordinate decisions about coastal and ocean use in a comprehensive plan. CMSP utilizes information about the location of significant human activities, marine resources, and other key data to determine the most suitable locations for particular uses.

Why is Washington beginning to use CMSP?

CMSP is anticipated to better coordinate management decisions among local, state, federal and tribal governments, and community stakeholders. It will reduce user conflicts, environmental impacts, and address emerging uses of the marine environment.

Why Should You Participate in CMSP?

Successful CMSP depends upon participation of stakeholders like you. Spatial and economic information on recreational uses is crucial – these uses provide benefits to coast communities and to WA state, and it is important they are captured in CMSP decisions.

Surfrider Mission, CMSP, and You

The Surfrider Foundation is a grassroots environmental organization dedicated to the protection and enjoyment of the world’s oceans, waves, and beaches. Through our network of seventy-five chapters and membership of thousands of recreational ocean users, we are working to support effective CMSP.

We seek to collect data on recreational uses, and we have the capacity to foster the participation of recreational users in the CMSP process. Our organization represents a wide range of recreational users including: beach-goers, surfers, kayakers, wildlife viewers, divers, boaters, etc.
