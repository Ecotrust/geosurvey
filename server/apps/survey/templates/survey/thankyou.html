Frequently Asked Questions

Who is conducting this study?

The Surfrider Foundation is conducting the study in partnership with Ecotrust, Monmouth University’s Urban Coast Institute, and The Nature Conservancy.

Who should fill out the survey?

Beach goers, swimmers, beach wildlife viewers, surfers, kayakers, windsurfers, divers, and other “non-consumptive” recreational users should complete the survey. Separate studies are focusing on uses like fishing and boating.

How do I participate?

Participation is simple: go to http://www.surfrider.org/mid-atlantic-recreation and enter your email address. A unique survey link will then be sent to you via email. You can then fill out the survey at your own pace, saving as you go.

Why should I participate?

Your help is needed to ensure that your favorite recreation areas are considered in coastal and ocean planning. Recreational uses are poorly mapped and data is limited. To minimize conflict and maximize efficiency between different coastal and ocean uses, MARCO is developing an online mapping tool to support ocean planning in the Mid-Atlantic region. To ensure that the interests and safety of recreational users are considered in management plans, better information is needed.

What is ocean planning?

As new coastal and ocean uses develop, it is becoming clear to resource managers that an over-arching organizational plan is needed. Currently areas are managed by a patchwork of state and federal agencies. Ocean planning will allow different uses like fishing, diving, and shipping to coexist by designating specific areas for them, not unlike land-based zoning.

Will my favorite coastal or ocean recreation spot be revealed to everyone else?

No, the data used to produce spatial maps will be aggregated so as to not reveal individual locations. The data will not be posted on recreational user websites or shared with travel guides, etc.

What is MARCO and how does it relate to this study?

The Mid-Atlantic Regional Council on the Ocean, or MARCO, is an arrangement by the Governors of New York, New Jersey, Delaware, Maryland, and Virginia to collaborate in advancing goals to protect our ocean and coast. To further these goals, MARCO created the Ocean Data Portal, an online tool to organize available data from the region. The data from this study will be placed on the Ocean Data Portal. MARCO is also organizing a similar study to this one, focused solely on recreational boating.
